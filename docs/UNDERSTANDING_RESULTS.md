# Understanding Your SEG-Y Analysis Results

**A Practical Guide to Interpreting Seismic Analysis Reports**

---

## 📋 What This Guide Covers

When you run our SEG-Y analysis tools, you'll receive detailed reports. This guide explains what those results mean in plain language - no geophysics degree required!

---

## 🎯 The Big Picture: What Analysis Tells You

### What Seismic Analysis Can Do
✅ **Identify potential resources** (oil, gas, minerals, water)
✅ **Map geological structures** (rock layers, faults, domes)
✅ **Estimate rock properties** (hardness, fluid content)
✅ **Guide drilling decisions** (where to drill, where not to)
✅ **Reduce exploration risk** (avoid expensive dry holes)

### What Seismic Analysis Cannot Do
❌ **Guarantee what's underground** (only drilling can confirm)
❌ **Replace geological expertise** (local knowledge matters)
❌ **Perfect accuracy** (expect 60-90% confidence for major features)
❌ **Detect every detail** (some features are too small to see)

## 📊 Reading Your Analysis Report

### Report Sections Explained

#### 1. Executive Summary
**What it tells you**: The bottom line - what did we find?

**Key terms to understand**:
- **Prospect**: An area worth investigating further (not guaranteed!)
- **Confidence**: How sure we are (High/Medium/Low)
- **Recommendation**: What we suggest you do next

**Example**:
> *"Found 3 high-confidence prospects. Recommended for further study."*

**Translation**: We found 3 interesting areas with strong evidence. Consider drilling or doing more detailed surveys.

---

#### 2. Prospect Details
**What it tells you**: Specific information about each target

**Location Information**:
- **Inline/Crossline**: Like map coordinates for the seismic data
- **Depth**: How deep the target is (in meters or feet)
- **Two-way time**: How long sound takes to reach it and bounce back

**Quality Metrics**:
- **Signal quality**: Is the data clear or noisy?
- **Amplitude strength**: How strong is the reflection?
- **Consistency**: Does the feature look real or could it be an artifact?

**Example**:
> *"Prospect A: Inline 150-200, Crossline 80-120, Depth 2500m, High amplitude anomaly, Good signal quality"*

**Translation**: At this location (defined by grid coordinates), 2.5km underground, there's a strong signal that might indicate something interesting. The data quality is good, so we trust this result.

---

#### 3. Geological Indicators
**What it tells you**: What geological features suggest resources

**Common Indicators**:

**Bright Spot** (most famous for oil/gas)
- **What it is**: An unusually strong reflection
- **Why it happens**: Gas in rock changes how sound waves travel
- **Reliability**: Good for shallow gas, less reliable for deep targets
- **False positives**: Can be caused by hard rock layers, basalt flows

**Flat Spot** (very reliable)
- **What it is**: A flat reflection cutting through angled rock layers
- **Why it happens**: Fluid contacts (gas-oil, oil-water) stay flat
- **Reliability**: Very high - fluids always settle flat due to gravity
- **What it means**: Almost certainly indicates a fluid contact

**Dim Spot** (subtle but important)
- **What it is**: An unusually weak reflection
- **Why it happens**: Rock properties match surrounding rock
- **Reliability**: Moderate - can indicate oil or tight rock
- **Needs**: Careful calibration with known data

**AVO Anomaly** (requires offset data)
- **What it is**: Amplitude changes with viewing angle
- **Why it happens**: Different fluids reflect differently at angles
- **Reliability**: High when done correctly
- **Used for**: Distinguishing gas from oil from water

---

#### 4. Risk Assessment
**What it tells you**: What could go wrong?

**Geological Risk**:
- **Complex geology**: Folded, faulted rock is harder to interpret
- **Depth uncertainty**: We might be off by ±100m or more
- **Structural integrity**: The trap might leak

**Data Quality Risk**:
- **Poor signal-to-noise**: Hard to see real features
- **Processing artifacts**: False features created by processing
- **Inadequate coverage**: Gaps in the data

**Economic Risk**:
- **Size too small**: Not enough resource to be profitable
- **Too deep**: Too expensive to drill
- **Infrastructure**: Remote locations cost more to develop

**Example Risk Statement**:
> *"Medium geological risk due to faulting. Low data quality risk. High economic risk due to depth (>4000m)."*

**Translation**: The geology is somewhat complex (faulted), but our data is good. However, it's very deep, making it expensive to drill and potentially not profitable.

---

#### 5. Recommendations
**What it tells you**: What should you do next?

**Common Recommendations**:

**"Recommended for drilling"**
- Multiple strong indicators
- Acceptable risk levels
- Economically viable size
- Ready for the next phase

**"Further study recommended"**
- Interesting but inconclusive
- Need more or better data
- Consider 3D seismic or detailed surveys
- Not ready to drill yet

**"Not recommended"**
- Low probability of success
- High risk, low reward
- Better targets available
- Save your money for better prospects

**"Acquire additional data"**
- Need more information
- Current data insufficient
- Specific surveys recommended

## 🎨 Understanding Visualizations

### Amplitude Maps
**What they show**: Signal strength across an area

**Reading the map**:
- **Red/Orange**: Strong signals (possible hydrocarbons)
- **Blue/Green**: Weak signals (probably normal rock)
- **Patterns**: Look for consistent shapes, not random spots

**What to look for**:
- Bright spots with consistent shapes
- Anomalies that follow geological structures
- Size of the anomaly (bigger is usually better)

---

### Time Slices
**What they show**: A horizontal slice at a specific depth/time

**Reading the slice**:
- Like looking at a map at one depth level
- Shows how features connect horizontally
- Reveals the shape and size of anomalies

**What to look for**:
- Continuous features (not just dots)
- Consistent patterns
- Relationship to known geology

---

### Cross Sections
**What they show**: A vertical slice through the earth

**Reading the section**:
- Vertical view from surface down to target depth
- Shows rock layers and structures
- Like looking at a slice of cake from the side

**What to look for**:
- Layer continuity
- Faults and breaks
- Trap configurations (domes, pinch-outs)
- Bright spots, flat spots, dim spots

## 🔢 Interpreting Numbers and Values

### Amplitude Values
**What they mean**: Strength of the reflected signal

**Typical ranges**:
- **Background**: -10 to +10 (arbitrary units)
- **Weak anomaly**: 2-3x background
- **Strong anomaly**: 5-10x background
- **Exceptional**: >10x background

**What to remember**:
- Relative values matter more than absolute
- Compare to surrounding area
- Consistent over area = more reliable

---

### Depth Estimates
**What they mean**: How deep the target is

**Accuracy considerations**:
- **Shallow (<2000m)**: ±50-100m accuracy
- **Medium (2000-4000m)**: ±100-200m accuracy
- **Deep (>4000m)**: ±200-500m accuracy

**Why it matters**:
- Deeper = more expensive to drill
- Depth affects temperature and pressure
- Impacts equipment selection

---

### Confidence Levels
**What they mean**: How reliable are the results?

**High Confidence** (>70%):
- Multiple indicators agree
- Good data quality
- Simple geology
- Similar to known successful cases

**Medium Confidence** (40-70%):
- Some indicators present
- Decent data quality
- Moderate geological complexity
- Needs verification

**Low Confidence** (<40%):
- Weak or conflicting indicators
- Poor data quality
- Complex geology
- High uncertainty

## 🎯 Making Decisions Based on Results

### For Exploration Managers

**Question**: Should we drill here?

**Consider**:
1. **Confidence level** - Is the evidence strong?
2. **Risk tolerance** - Can we afford a dry hole?
3. **Economic potential** - If successful, is it profitable?
4. **Alternatives** - Are there better targets?

**Decision framework**:
- **High confidence + Good economics** → Green light
- **Medium confidence + Great economics** → Consider
- **Low confidence + Poor economics** → Pass

---

### For Researchers

**Question**: Is this worth studying further?

**Consider**:
1. **Scientific interest** - Does this advance knowledge?
2. **Data quality** - Can we learn from this?
3. **Novelty** - Is this something new or unusual?
4. **Publications** - Could this lead to papers?

**Decision framework**:
- **Interesting features + Good data** → Study further
- **Unusual geology + Questions** → Investigate
- **Routine geology + Poor data** → Lower priority

---

### For Students

**Question**: What can I learn from this?

**Educational value**:
1. **Compare interpretation** - What do you see vs. what the report says?
2. **Understand uncertainty** - Where might the analysis be wrong?
3. **Learn terminology** - Look up unfamiliar terms
4. **Practice skills** - Try interpreting yourself first

**Learning approach**:
- Review the report
- Look at the data yourself
- See if you agree
- Understand why interpretations differ

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Overconfidence
❌ **Don't**: Treat the analysis as gospel truth
✅ **Do**: Remember it's a guide, not a guarantee

### Ignoring Context
❌ **Don't**: Look at one indicator in isolation
✅ **Do**: Consider multiple lines of evidence

### Forgetting Economics
❌ **Don't**: Get excited about technical success
✅ **Do**: Ask if it's economically viable

### Ignoring Risk
❌ **Don't**: Focus only on upside potential
✅ **Do**: Consider what could go wrong

### Cherry-Picking
❌ **Don't**: Only look at positive indicators
✅ **Do**: Consider all evidence, positive and negative

## 📈 Improving Confidence in Results

### Want to be more sure? Additional work can help:

**Acquire More Data**:
- **3D seismic**: Much better than 2D for detail
- **Reprocessing**: Better algorithms can reveal more
- **Attribute analysis**: Additional processing techniques

**Ground Truth**:
- **Well logs**: Drill a test hole to calibrate
- **Production data**: Check if nearby wells were successful
- **Outcrop analogs**: Study similar exposed rock layers

**Expert Review**:
- **Multiple interpreters**: Get different perspectives
- **Specialist analysis**: Hire experts for complex areas
- **Peer review**: Have others check your work

**Time Integration**:
- **Monitor results**: Learn from drilling outcomes
- **Update interpretations**: Incorporate new data
- **Refine models**: Improve understanding over time

## 💬 Questions to Ask About Your Results

### Technical Questions:
- How confident are we in this interpretation?
- What are the main sources of uncertainty?
- Could there be alternative explanations?
- How does this compare to similar successful cases?

### Practical Questions:
- What additional data would improve confidence?
- What are the next steps if we proceed?
- What are the costs of next steps?
- What are the timelines for further work?

### Business Questions:
- What's the probability of success?
- What's the potential size if successful?
- What are the costs to drill/explore?
- What are the risks and how do we mitigate them?
